# First Session Experience Audit

## Scope

This is a review of the current Two Second Witness 4.0 first-session experience after adding the future ecosystem placeholders. No final gameplay or artwork was added in this pass.

## Current journey

```text
ITTYBITTYBITES activation
→ Two Second Witness identity
→ Iris awakening
→ voice/visual invitation
→ center tap
→ Story Mode placeholder
→ first chapter focus point
→ production Witness flow
→ production challenge rooms
→ Iris return
→ Discover constellation
```

## Findings

### App identity

**PASS:** The activation layer now presents ITTYBITTYBITES and Two Second Witness before the Iris.

**Concern:** The user may still perceive the Iris as the product if the startup identity fades too quickly or the first Iris state does not retain the Two Second Witness context. Product identity should remain present in small, non-dashboard brand language.

### Iris as central home

**PASS:** The Iris is the visible root and all current destinations are reached through its transition/navigation model.

**PASS:** Center tap now opens the Story Mode placeholder before production Witness, which aligns the center action with the new product hierarchy.

### Story Mode priority

**PASS:** Story Mode has a central focus point and a Rank 1 / Observer framing.

**Concern:** It is still a placeholder and currently hands directly into the existing recommended production Witness flow. It does not yet show a real Rank Chapter or moment sequence.

### Daily Witness

**PASS:** Discover exposes a clearly named Daily Witness destination with today/readiness, streak, and personal-best language.

**Concern:** It is intentionally nonfunctional and says the daily moment is not yet built. This is acceptable for the placeholder foundation but should not be mistaken for a shipping feature.

### Weekly Investigation

**PASS:** Discover exposes a clearly named Weekly Investigation destination with case/progress/archive-reward language.

**Concern:** It is intentionally nonfunctional and does not yet establish a real weekly schedule or resume state.

### Your Iris

**PASS:** Discover includes a Your Iris point, and the placeholder explains Rank, Mastery, Discoveries, Achievements, Records, and Calibration.

**Concern:** Down still opens the production Profile room while Discover Your Iris opens the future placeholder. This is useful for preserving production functionality but creates a temporary two-step understanding of the personal space.

### Old challenge concepts

**PASS:** Center no longer opens a challenge-family selection menu.

**Concern:** The production Challenge Library remains visible inside Archive, which is correct as a secondary advanced room. Family names may still appear before the Story Mode layer is fully implemented in that room.

## First-session comprehension assessment

A new user should now understand:

- the Iris is the gateway;
- center leads to a journey rather than a family menu;
- right reveals additional future experiences;
- Daily and Weekly are ongoing personal activities;
- Your Iris is a personal/progression space.

The remaining comprehension risk is that Story Mode, Daily, Weekly, and Your Iris are visibly placeholders rather than complete experiences. This is expected at the current foundation stage.

## Priority recommendations

1. Keep Story Mode visually dominant and central.
2. Keep Daily/Weekly points quieter than Story Mode.
3. Keep Your Iris as one future personal-space concept while production Profile/Settings remain accessible.
4. Do not expose a challenge family list during first session.
5. When Story Mode is implemented, ensure the first production challenge is framed as a Witness Moment, not a standalone mode.
