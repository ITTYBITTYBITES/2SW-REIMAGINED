# Your Iris — Personal Hub Design

## Concept

“Your Iris” is the player’s unified personal space. It should feel like looking at the growth and calibration of an instrument, not opening a profile/settings dashboard.

The current production Profile and Settings rooms remain authoritative and accessible. This document defines the future unified presentation boundary.

## Sections

### Witness Rank

- current rank name;
- progress toward next rank;
- current Rank Chapter;
- unlocked future locations;
- next perception ability.

Initial rank language:

1. Observer
2. Investigator
3. Archivist

### Mastery

Show family skills as perception capabilities:

- Observation;
- Memory;
- Discovery/change detection;
- Accuracy;
- Consistency/streak.

Family-specific production mastery remains available in detail.

### Discoveries

Show evidence collected from production result metadata:

- completed Witness Moments;
- evidence highlights;
- scenario/context;
- date/history;
- replay entry where supported.

### Achievements

Show production achievements as milestones in the player’s relationship with noticing. Keep `AchievementService` as the data authority.

### Personal Records

Show:

- best performances;
- fastest response where meaningful;
- current/best streak;
- family mastery;
- completed chapters;
- history.

### Calibration

Use production `SettingsService` and `AccessibilityService` for:

- audio;
- VoiceGuide;
- haptics;
- motion/reduced motion;
- parallax;
- high contrast/color assistance;
- captions;
- controls/input;
- orientation/display.

## Presentation rules

- One spatial personal instrument, not a dashboard.
- Sections emerge as orbiting records, layers, or instrument readouts.
- Explicit access path may use conventional labeled controls.
- Default view should be calm and visually summarized.
- No second profile/save/progression system.
