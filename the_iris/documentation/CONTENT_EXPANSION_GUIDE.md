# Content Expansion Guide

## Goal

Grow Two Second Witness from its first Story Mode moments to 100, 500, and 1000+ Witness Moments without losing its identity.

## Identity safeguards

Every new moment must preserve:

- the Witness Moment loop;
- Iris entry/return;
- fair observation;
- meaningful discovery;
- evidence-grounded reveal;
- production quality standards;
- accessibility and sound-off comprehension;
- Archive/memory continuity;
- no challenge-family-first navigation.

## 100 Moments

### Organization

- 10 Rank Chapters of approximately 10 moments;
- chapter themes balance history, science, art, mystery, engineering, exploration, emotion, everyday moments, impossible moments, and future moments;
- one reference visual/interaction vocabulary per chapter;
- each moment has a distinct emotional arc and observation shape.

### Production model

- small curated authoring team;
- hand-reviewed scenarios;
- reusable environment/object libraries;
- controlled Future Witness Mechanics containers;
- manual emotional/reveal review;
- production family mechanics used only as internal support where appropriate.

### Quality gate

Every moment receives:

- creative brief;
- moment template;
- accessibility review;
- fairness/content validation;
- evidence/reveal review;
- replay/Archive mapping;
- human playtest.

## 500 Moments

### Organization

- 25–50 Rank Chapters or chapter arcs;
- content families by setting/material/era/phenomenon;
- local Daily/Weekly selection pools;
- linked moment threads and recurring Archive clues;
- Director variety and repeat prevention become mandatory.

### Production model

- standardized content authoring tools;
- schema validation and preview generation;
- asset batch production;
- reusable lighting/material/environment kits;
- localized narrative and accessibility content;
- automated deterministic generation/fairness tests.

### Quality safeguards

- per-chapter variation budget;
- emotional tone balance;
- no overproduction of crime/horror/mystery content;
- content-versioned saves/replays;
- Archive search/organization without making it the primary home.

## 1000+ Moments

### Organization

- modular Story Chapters;
- linked regional/temporal/subject collections;
- dynamic but deterministic Director selection;
- authored anchors plus generated variations;
- long-term Archive and mastery views;
- seasonal/weekly content only when local/offline rules remain clear.

### Production model

- content pipeline owned by data contracts;
- family/interaction mechanics as platform modules;
- moment metadata authored separately from assets;
- reusable scene/evidence/voice/audio components;
- automated content QA and performance budgets;
- independent content versioning and rollback.

### Identity safeguard at scale

Scale must increase the variety of things worth noticing, not simply the number of puzzles. A thousand moments should feel like an expanding world of perception, not a thousand rows in a challenge list.

## Content metadata requirements

Every moment should define:

- ID/version;
- chapter/rank;
- setting/theme;
- emotional tone;
- observation focus;
- investigation/discovery focus;
- evidence/reveal;
- reflection;
- asset/audio/voice references;
- accessibility requirements;
- difficulty context;
- replay/mastery variants;
- Archive record mapping;
- localization requirements.

## Asset expansion rules

- Use replaceable versioned assets.
- Keep scene/content logic separate from textures/audio.
- Avoid one-off scripts for ordinary content.
- Add content through manifests where a contract already exists.
- Do not delete old assets until save/replay and roadmap references are checked.
- Keep Android texture/audio budgets explicit.

## Mechanical expansion rules

A genuinely new mechanic may be added only when:

- existing future mechanic containers cannot express the intended experience;
- the mechanic supports a distinct perception ability;
- it has an accessibility path;
- it has deterministic/fair validation;
- it can produce evidence/reveal;
- it has a replay/mastery role;
- it does not force a new top-level product category.

## Content governance

- Creative Director approves identity/emotional direction.
- Game Design approves fairness/interaction.
- Narrative approves framing/sensitivity.
- UX approves discoverability/accessibility.
- Technical Design approves contracts/performance.
- Production QA approves device/runtime behavior.

## Failure mode to avoid

Do not optimize for the count of moments at the expense of the memory each moment leaves. The target is a larger world, not a larger menu.
