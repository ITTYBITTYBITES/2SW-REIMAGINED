**Campaign:** Architecture Convergence  
**Status:** Approved & Authored (Post-MISSION 015 Calibration)

---

## 1. Purpose & Scope

This document codifies the permanent design grammar of **Two Second Witness**. Established through the successful execution and calibration of MISSION 014 and MISSION 015, this grammar governs all future incident authoring (WM_002 onward) to ensure every new moment reinforces the cognitive instrument's core mental model without reinventing onboarding.

### Core Mental Model (Target Player Understanding)
> *"I am examining a memory, reconstructing what matters, and using perspectives to understand what changed."*

---

## 2. Observation Rules (The 2-Second Cinematic)

### What Players Should Notice
- Spatial layout, dominant lighting, central focal anchors, and subtle ambient motion (e.g., dust motes, light refraction, vapor).
- The observation window is **not** a pixel-hunt or hidden-object search. It is an intentional optical exposure.

### How Clues Are Presented
- **Primary Anchor:** Immediate visual context (e.g., the artist loft desk, the paused hand).
- **Subtle Layer:** Secondary environmental detail that interacts with light or time (e.g., the crystal prism, the prism spectrum beam).
- **Duration:** Exactly 2.0 seconds, accompanied by a focus notice tone and subtle haptic feedback, creating curiosity rather than frustration.

---

## 3. Reconstruction Rules (Carrying Memory)

### What Fragments Represent
- Fragments (`fragment_palette`) are **memory anchors**, not inventory items or puzzle pieces.
- Placing a fragment onto a ghost outline answers: *"What part of the memory am I rebuilding?"*
- There are no incorrect placements or failed validation states; the player builds the memory from what they carried. Desk fidelity and background clarity scale dynamically with placement progress, providing intuitive tactile feedback.

---

## 4. Investigation Rules (Multi-Perspective Truth)

### How Attunements Reveal Layers
- Tapping hotspots in the frozen moment triggers **attunements** (spectral, forensic, trajectory, text, thermal).
- Attunements must feel like **discovery tools**, not answer buttons.
- Each attunement peels back a different perspective of the memory, proving why the observed detail mattered (e.g., refraction angles, charcoal underdrawings, margin notes).
- Discovering the threshold (e.g., 3 attunements) prompts Iris intervention, rewarding curiosity without forcing a mechanical checklist.

---

## 5. Revelation Rules (The Emotional Payoff)

### How Truth Is Presented
- The revelation phase dynamically assembles the permanent **Archive Entry** from the player's carried fragments and completed attunements.
- Stepwise reveal pacing allows the player to digest what they noticed, what they investigated, and Iris's final reflection note.
- The outcome reinforces emotional closure: *"I did not find a mistake. I preserved a memory."*

---

## 6. Failure & Grace Rules

### How Mistakes Teach Rather Than Punish
- The cognitive instrument does not punish hesitation or incomplete observation with game-overs.
- The grace window (e.g., 2.8-second reflection tone upon moment completion) allows the emotional resonance to settle before automatically returning the player to Iris.
- Failure (if triggered by abandoning or failing a phase) is handled gracefully through IncidentRegistry lifecycle management, ensuring the player always feels guided by Iris back into presence.
