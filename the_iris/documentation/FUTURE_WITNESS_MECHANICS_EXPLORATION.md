# Future Witness Mechanics Exploration

**Status:** concept exploration only
**Implementation:** none
**Product:** Two Second Witness 4.0 Story Mode

## Design premise

The player should not feel that they selected or played a memory game, hidden-object game, trivia game, or puzzle mode. They should feel that they witnessed an event and are trying to understand what it means.

Each candidate below is a possible internal mechanic for a future Witness Moment. None is a final challenge type. A mechanic is successful only when it supports the moment’s story, rewards attention, and produces an understandable discovery.

## Scoring key

Each candidate receives six 1–10 scores:

- **W:** feels like being a Witness;
- **P:** avoids feeling like a traditional puzzle game;
- **C:** supports cinematic storytelling;
- **M:** works on mobile;
- **S:** scales to hundreds of moments;
- **A:** supports accessibility.

Scores are directional design scores, not test results.

# 1. Observation mechanics

## O1 — Attention Aperture

- **Player fantasy:** Looking through a living lens until the meaningful layer comes into focus.
- **Physical action:** Touch/hold or mouse-focus a region while the Iris gently contracts the field; release when the player feels the field is understood.
- **Witness fit:** Attention itself is the action, not a button puzzle.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Smaller focus regions, more competing movement, shorter stable windows, layered depth.
- **Mobile:** Excellent; natural hold/focus interaction.
- **Replay:** Different focus targets, optional hidden layers, mastery of calm precision.
- **Scores:** W9 / P9 / C9 / M9 / S9 / A8.

## O2 — Moving Detail

- **Player fantasy:** Following one ordinary thing through a living environment.
- **Physical action:** Drag gaze/finger or move pointer along a subtle motion path without chasing unrelated movement.
- **Witness fit:** The player witnesses continuity and notices when the detail diverges.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Speed, occlusion, path complexity, decoy movement, pause timing.
- **Mobile:** Strong if movement stays low-amplitude and target size is generous.
- **Replay:** Alternate paths, different causal events, hidden route variations.
- **Scores:** W9 / P8 / C10 / M8 / S9 / A7.

## O3 — Peripheral Drift

- **Player fantasy:** Realizing that something important happened outside the center of attention.
- **Physical action:** Attend to a central field while subtle peripheral events occur; later shift attention toward a cue.
- **Witness fit:** Directly explores what people normally miss.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Peripheral contrast, event timing, number of zones, cue subtlety.
- **Mobile:** Good with visible edge cues and non-precision interaction.
- **Replay:** Different peripheral events and attention priorities.
- **Scores:** W10 / P9 / C9 / M8 / S9 / A7.

## O4 — Perspective Tilt

- **Player fantasy:** Looking at the same place from another physical or emotional angle.
- **Physical action:** Drag a lens/orbit around a scene or tilt a virtual viewing plane; no motion sensor required.
- **Witness fit:** Perception changes when viewpoint changes.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Number of perspectives, parallax depth, amount of occlusion, viewpoint order.
- **Mobile:** Good with drag; avoid mandatory device movement.
- **Replay:** Alternate viewpoints reveal different evidence.
- **Scores:** W9 / P8 / C10 / M8 / S8 / A8.

## O5 — Rhythm of Presence

- **Player fantasy:** Sensing a place through intervals of presence and absence.
- **Physical action:** Watch/listen to a sequence of environmental pulses and identify the moment where the rhythm changes.
- **Witness fit:** Observational and atmospheric rather than answer-first.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Interval similarity, silence duration, number of pulses, multimodal ambiguity.
- **Mobile:** Good; touch is optional.
- **Replay:** Procedural rhythm patterns and narrative soundscapes.
- **Scores:** W8 / P9 / C9 / M9 / S9 / A7.

## O6 — Stillness Window

- **Player fantasy:** Becoming still enough for a hidden event to reveal itself.
- **Physical action:** Hold the phone/finger/cursor within a forgiving calm zone; motion quiets the field instead of acting as a precision test.
- **Witness fit:** Stillness is a perceptual choice.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Required stillness duration, ambient distraction, focus zone size.
- **Mobile:** Strong but must support reduced-motion and motor accommodations.
- **Replay:** Different secrets emerge after different kinds of patience.
- **Scores:** W9 / P9 / C8 / M8 / S8 / A6.

## O7 — Environmental Listening

- **Player fantasy:** Noticing a place through its sound and visual response.
- **Physical action:** Identify the direction/relationship of audio-visual cues by moving attention, not by selecting an audio answer.
- **Witness fit:** Feels like witnessing an unseen space.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Layer count, sound similarity, spatial separation, optional audio.
- **Mobile:** Good with visual equivalent when sound is unavailable.
- **Replay:** New soundscapes and hidden source relationships.
- **Scores:** W9 / P8 / C10 / M8 / S8 / A6.

## O8 — Change of Light

- **Player fantasy:** Seeing a place change under a passing light or time shift.
- **Physical action:** Sweep a light/focus plane across a scene and notice what becomes visible.
- **Witness fit:** Light becomes an instrument of observation.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Shadow density, subtlety of revealed details, sweep speed, decoys.
- **Mobile:** Excellent with drag or automated motion.
- **Replay:** Different lighting conditions reveal alternate truths.
- **Scores:** W9 / P9 / C10 / M9 / S9 / A8.

## O9 — Attention Handoff

- **Player fantasy:** Realizing that one detail is asking you to notice another.
- **Physical action:** Follow a chain of optical cues from one region to the next without a visible route line.
- **Witness fit:** Teaches relational attention.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Chain length, cue delay, decoy cues, branching.
- **Mobile:** Good if each handoff is target-forgiving.
- **Replay:** Different causal/evidence chains.
- **Scores:** W10 / P9 / C9 / M8 / S10 / A7.

## O10 — Witnessed Absence

- **Player fantasy:** Recognizing that something is missing from an otherwise familiar place.
- **Physical action:** Observe a field and later indicate which presence/relationship feels incomplete, without a conventional hidden-object search.
- **Witness fit:** Absence is a discovery, not a collectible hunt.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Similarity, number of plausible absences, relationship complexity.
- **Mobile:** Good with accessible choice or focus.
- **Replay:** Different absences and narrative meanings.
- **Scores:** W10 / P8 / C9 / M8 / S9 / A8.

# 2. Memory Reconstruction mechanics

## M1 — Temporal Echo

- **Player fantasy:** Rebuilding a short moment from fragments of what happened first, next, and last.
- **Physical action:** Place/tune fragments along an event ribbon, with forgiving reorder interaction.
- **Witness fit:** Memory is treated as time, not matching cards.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Fragment count, ambiguity, near-simultaneous events, missing steps.
- **Mobile:** Good with drag and accessible ordering adapter.
- **Replay:** Alternate timelines, new fragment sets, partial memories.
- **Scores:** W9 / P8 / C10 / M8 / S10 / A7.

## M2 — Relationship Map

- **Player fantasy:** Remembering which things belonged together.
- **Physical action:** Connect a small number of witness fragments by relationship type.
- **Witness fit:** Understanding relationships is more meaningful than remembering isolated objects.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Node count, relationship ambiguity, distractor links, hidden relationship types.
- **Mobile:** Good if graph size stays small and links are forgiving.
- **Replay:** New relational structures and story consequences.
- **Scores:** W9 / P7 / C9 / M7 / S9 / A6.

## M3 — Emotional Trace

- **Player fantasy:** Remembering the feeling or tension of a moment without turning it into a personality quiz.
- **Physical action:** Select or arrange sensory/visual traces that best reflect the observed shift, with evidence-grounded options.
- **Witness fit:** Adds understanding while avoiding “correct emotion” claims.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Number of cues, ambiguity, layered interpretations, evidence support.
- **Mobile:** Good with single-choice or accessible ordered selection.
- **Replay:** Alternate interpretations can be unlocked and compared.
- **Scores:** W9 / P8 / C10 / M9 / S8 / A8.

## M4 — Fragment Assembly

- **Player fantasy:** Restoring a memory that has broken into visual, spatial, or auditory fragments.
- **Physical action:** Move fragments into a perceptually coherent arrangement; no rigid jigsaw requirement.
- **Witness fit:** Reconstructs meaning rather than an image.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Fragment count, missing fragment, orientation, competing assemblies.
- **Mobile:** Good with drag or keyboard ordering; needs accessible alternative.
- **Replay:** Different fragment arrangements and incomplete memories.
- **Scores:** W9 / P7 / C9 / M8 / S9 / A6.

## M5 — Witnessed Sequence

- **Player fantasy:** Remembering a sequence of changes rather than a row of symbols.
- **Physical action:** Tap/select the moments in the order they were witnessed.
- **Witness fit:** Keeps sequence memory attached to an event.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Length, interval, reversals, simultaneous events.
- **Mobile:** Strong.
- **Replay:** Variant sequences and alternate evidence paths.
- **Scores:** W8 / P8 / C8 / M9 / S9 / A8.

## M6 — Spatial Imprint

- **Player fantasy:** Remembering where a meaningful presence sat in a space.
- **Physical action:** Place a marker or trace a region on a simplified memory field.
- **Witness fit:** Spatial memory remains contextual, not a spot-the-difference tap.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Grid/continuous space, target size, occlusion, multiple relationships.
- **Mobile:** Good with normalized targets and accessible region options.
- **Replay:** New scenes and changed spatial meanings.
- **Scores:** W8 / P8 / C8 / M8 / S9 / A7.

## M7 — Soundless Recall

- **Player fantasy:** Remembering a visual event even after the sound/voice has gone.
- **Physical action:** Reconstruct visual markers that corresponded to an audio-visual moment; audio is optional.
- **Witness fit:** Ensures sound enhances but does not define memory.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Cue count, temporal overlap, visual similarity.
- **Mobile:** Good with visual alternatives.
- **Replay:** Multimodal versions and accessibility variants.
- **Scores:** W8 / P8 / C9 / M8 / S8 / A9.

## M8 — What Was Not Said

- **Player fantasy:** Inferring the missing part of a witnessed interaction from observable evidence.
- **Physical action:** Choose/assemble the most evidence-supported missing beat.
- **Witness fit:** Understanding is grounded in observation, not trivia.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Evidence ambiguity, relationship count, interpretation space.
- **Mobile:** Good with explicit choice and captions.
- **Replay:** Alternative interpretations and hidden context.
- **Scores:** W10 / P8 / C10 / M9 / S9 / A8.

## M9 — Memory Confidence

- **Player fantasy:** Knowing which parts of your memory are certain and which are uncertain.
- **Physical action:** Mark/weight fragments by confidence before evidence is shown.
- **Witness fit:** Makes the act of remembering visible.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Number of fragments, confidence ambiguity, consequence of uncertainty.
- **Mobile:** Good; can use simple taps or sliders.
- **Replay:** Compare confidence patterns across attempts.
- **Scores:** W9 / P9 / C8 / M9 / S8 / A8.

## M10 — Revisit the Moment

- **Player fantasy:** Returning to a remembered scene and selecting what deserves another look.
- **Physical action:** Navigate a memory replay with limited focus opportunities.
- **Witness fit:** Makes memory active and reflective, not a static answer.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Replay length, number of focus opportunities, hidden evidence.
- **Mobile:** Excellent if focus is forgiving.
- **Replay:** Different overlooked details and alternate routes.
- **Scores:** W10 / P9 / C10 / M8 / S9 / A8.

# 3. Investigation mechanics

- **Physical action:** Pan/focus a lens over permitted evidence regions.

- **Difficulty scaling:** Region count, subtle evidence, lens constraints.

- **Replay:** Alternate evidence layers.
- **Scores:** W10 / P9 / C9 / M9 / S9 / A8.

## I2 — Perspective Replay

- **Player fantasy:** Replaying the same moment from another witness position.
- **Physical action:** Scrub/choose a short alternate viewpoint or timeline.
- **Witness fit:** Investigation is perspective, not object collection.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Viewpoint count, differences, chronology, ambiguity.
- **Mobile:** Good with simple scrubber or discrete views.
- **Replay:** Multiple valid viewpoints and evidence trails.
- **Scores:** W10 / P9 / C10 / M8 / S9 / A8.

## I3 — Trace the Source

- **Physical action:** Follow a subtle visual/audio trace to its source.

- **Replay:** New source paths.
- **Scores:** W9 / P8 / C10 / M8 / S9 / A7.

## I5 — Material Examination

## I6 — Follow the Gaze

- **Witness fit:** Investigation becomes empathy and relational perception.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Gaze ambiguity, multiple agents, hidden context.

## I7 — Silent Interview

- **Witness fit:** The environment answers through observation.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Cue ambiguity, response layers, sequence of questions.

- **Scores:** W10 / P9 / C10 / M8 / S9 / A8.

- **Physical action:** Adjust a virtual light/focus control to reveal relationships.
- **Witness fit:** The instrument helps the player investigate.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Light constraint, hidden layers, energy/time budget.

- **Replay:** Alternate light reveals.
- **Scores:** W9 / P9 / C10 / M9 / S9 / A8.

## I9 — Evidence Selection by Meaning

- **Player fantasy:** Choosing the clue that actually explains the event, not the most obvious object.
- **Physical action:** Select among evidence fragments and see how the scene responds.
- **Witness fit:** Rewards understanding over visual hunting.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Plausible decoys, causal depth, evidence relationships.
- **Mobile:** Excellent with large choices.
- **Replay:** Multiple evidence paths.
- **Scores:** W10 / P8 / C9 / M9 / S9 / A8.

## I10 — Return to the Unfinished Detail

- **Player fantasy:** Revisiting the one thing that did not settle in memory.
- **Physical action:** Iris/scene directs player back to an unresolved region.
- **Witness fit:** Curiosity is the navigation mechanic.

- **Scores:** W9 / P9 / C10 / M9 / S9 / A9.

## R10 — Silence

- **Player fantasy:** Letting a moment remain unspoken.
- **Physical action:** Wait or choose to close without adding a reflection.
- **Witness fit:** Restraint becomes a valid response.
- **Difficulty scaling:** Timing/context only; should never be punitive.
- **Mobile:** Excellent.
- **Replay:** Different meaning through absence of testimony.
- **Scores:** W10 / P10 / C10 / M9 / S8 / A9.

# Recommended foundation mechanics

## Core Observation — O1 Attention Aperture

**Why:** It makes attention itself the primary act, maps naturally to the Iris, supports Story Mode framing, and works across scenes, memories, and evidence without looking like a mini-game.

## Core Memory — M1 Temporal Echo

**Why:** It converts recall into reconstructing meaning across time. It scales from a two-beat moment to a complex investigation and can use accessible ordering/choice adapters.

## Core Investigation — I6 Follow the Gaze

**Why:** It creates an embodied, relational investigation behavior. The player follows where another witness or object directs attention, which is more story-rich than scanning a grid.

## Core Discovery — D8 Memory Return

**Why:** It turns replay into a discovery mechanic. Returning to the same moment with new context supports long-term chapters and the Archive while avoiding repetition.

## Core Evidence — E5 Witness Chain

**Why:** It connects observation to understanding. A short truthful chain can be authored for hundreds of moments and produces a clear evidence reveal.

## Supporting Reflection — R8 Return Signal

**Why:** It ties the player’s reflection directly back to the Iris, making the interface feel like it remembers without introducing a chatbot or a parallel progression system.

## Foundation sequence

```text
Attention Aperture
→ Temporal Echo
→ Follow the Gaze
→ Witness Chain
→ Memory Return
→ Return Signal
```

These are recommended design foundations, not final locked challenge types. They still require prototype tests, accessibility review, content authoring experiments, and human validation before implementation.
