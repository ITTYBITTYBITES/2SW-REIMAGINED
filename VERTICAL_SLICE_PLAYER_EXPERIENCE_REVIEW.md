## 1. New Player Journey — Cold Launch → Awakening → Rank 1 → WM_001

### Entry (Cold Launch)

**What Happens:**
- Screen opens from pure darkness. No menus, no buttons, no instructions.
- The Living Iris slowly forms — stroma fibers materializing, pupil void emerging.
- Sub-bass respiration (48 Hz) swells. The eye *breathes*.

**Player Impression:**
The player does not land in a game. They land in an *experience*. There is no "Press Start." There is no logo splash followed by a main menu. The Iris awakens as the player awakens. This is immediately distinct from any mobile game in the market.

✅ **Verdict:** The cold launch establishes the core fantasy: "I am looking through something alive."

### Scene 1: Iris Awakening (~0s–15s)

**What Happens:**
- As the player moves their finger or cursor, the pupil executes ballistic saccadic snaps toward the pointer.
- Iris Voice: *"Attention is the beginning of memory."*

**Player Impression:**
The eye *responds* to the player's physical presence. No tutorial overlay explains this. The player discovers: "My attention moves the instrument." This teaches the fundamental grammar of the entire game — attention is the input mechanism — without a single line of instructional text.

✅ **Verdict:** Teaches core mechanic through direct sensation, not explanation.

### Scene 2: Looking Through the Lens (~15s–30s)

**What Happens:**
- When the player's gaze moves to center (pupil), the void clears to reveal a miniature memory preview — a desk scene.
- Iris Voice: *"Something was missed."*
- Title illuminates: **THE AWAKENING** / **TOUCH TO ENTER FIRST OBSERVATION**

**Player Impression:**
The pupil is not a blank void — it's a window. The player learns: "The center of the eye shows me places I can enter." This reframes the entire Iris from a visual effect to a navigable instrument.

⚠️ **Observation:** The title "THE AWAKENING" and prompt text appear as conventional UI labels. For a moment this magical, the typography could feel more integrated — perhaps etched into the optical field rather than displayed as floating text.

**Verdict:** Functional but could feel more organic. Minor polish opportunity.

### Scene 3: Mini Witness Moment (~30s–60s)

**What Happens:**
- Camera sweeps *through* the pupil ("The Threshold" transition — dilation + chromatic aberration).
- A 10-second memory unfolds: painter places brush near clock and canvas.
- Question appears: **WHAT CHANGED?** with three optical options.
- Selecting "THE REFLECTION SHIFTED" triggers a Phase Lock chord and reveals the hidden reflection.
- Iris Voice: *"The smallest detail can change the whole story."*

**Player Impression:**
This is the first time the player *does* something. The question format — "What changed?" — is deliberately different from "Find the difference" or "Spot the error." The player is not hunting for mistakes. They are noticing a subtle shift in light.

The reward for correct observation is not a score or a star. It is a *chord* — a resonant harmonic that fills the space. The player feels: "I heard the truth."

✅ **Verdict:** The mini moment successfully teaches: "I do not solve puzzles. I notice moments."

### Scene 4: Return & Rank 1 Unlock (~60s–75s)

**What Happens:**
- "The Blink" transition sweeps back to the Iris.
- Archive acceptance: *"The Archive has accepted your first observation."*
- Golden collarette filaments ignite around the pupil.
- First memory shard spawns in orbit.
- Banner: **RANK 1: OBSERVER** / *Chapter 1: Learning to Notice unlocked.*

**Player Impression:**
The Iris physically changed. The player's attention literally grew something inside the eye. The rank unlock is not a pop-up — it is a *transformation*. The player feels: "I did not earn a badge. I changed the instrument."

✅ **Verdict:** The rank reveal is emotional, not transactional.

### WM_001: The Unfinished Canvas (Entry)

**What Happens:**
- Center pupil portal updates from "The Awakening" to **CHAPTER 1: LEARNING TO NOTICE** with prompt **TOUCH TO ENTER WITNESS MOMENT**.
- Tapping center triggers "The Threshold" — camera sweeps into the moment.
- WitnessMomentOrchestrator begins the phase sequence.

**Player Impression:**
The transition from onboarding to Chapter 1 is seamless. There is no menu, no chapter select, no loading screen. The pupil portal simply *knows* where the player is in their journey.

✅ **Verdict:** The player flows from Awakening → Rank 1 → WM_001 without touching a single menu button.

## 2. Returning Player Journey — Launch → Iris → Current Chapter

### Entry (Cold Launch with Existing Profile)

**What Happens:**
- Instant entry into fully awake Living Iris (`awakening_level = 1.0`).
- No onboarding replay. No tutorial.
- Iris remembers: stroma shows progression (golden collarette, orbiting shards).
- Warm acoustic greeting (multi-harmonic recognition chord).

**Player Impression:**
The Iris *recognizes* them. There is no "Welcome back" pop-up, but the physical state of the eye communicates: "You have been here. You have changed me." The returning player feels ownership.

✅ **Verdict:** Zero friction re-entry. The eye *is* the save state.

### Navigation to Current Chapter

**What Happens:**
- Center pupil portal displays current chapter moment (e.g., **CHAPTER 1: THE FORGOTTEN MUSEUM (WM_002)**).
- If completed all Chapter 1 moments, portal alternates to **DAILY WITNESS**.
- Player taps center → "The Threshold" transition → directly into the next Witness Moment.

**Player Impression:**
There is no "Continue" button. There is no "Resume" dialog. The player's position in the journey is physically encoded in the eye's appearance. The next moment is *always* at the center of vision.

✅ **Verdict:** Returning players never navigate menus to continue their journey.

### Recent Activity Memory

**What Happens:**
- When returning from any screen to the Iris, `remember_recent_activity()` holds `recent_alert = 0.65` for 5.5 seconds.
- Directional rim sweep teaches spatial navigation for 3.4 seconds.

**Player Impression:**
The eye is *awake*. It does not droop back into idle. The player feels: "It knows I just came from somewhere."

✅ **Verdict:** Continuity of experience is maintained.

## 3. Core Experience Evaluation

### Is the Player Acting as a Witness?

**Yes.** Throughout both journeys, the player never:
- Clicks to solve a puzzle
- Hunts for differences in a spot-the-difference format
- Receives a score or grade
- Fails or loses

Instead, the player:
- Observes a 2-second moment (no input accepted)
- Reconstructs memory through spatial fragment placement (no validation)
- Investigates frozen moments through attunement (discovery, not extraction)
- Receives an archive entry that reflects *what they carried*

The word "puzzle" never appears. The word "score" never appears. The player's role is consistently that of a witness — someone who notices, carries, and preserves.

✅ **Verdict:** The Witness fantasy is intact and consistent.

### Is the Iris Guiding Without Explaining Too Much?

**Mostly yes.** The Iris speaks at critical junctures:
- *"Attention is the beginning of memory."* (Awakening — teaches core concept)
- *"Something was missed."* (Lens discovery — invites curiosity)
- *"The smallest detail can change the whole story."* (Mini moment — reveals grammar)
- *"Place what stayed with you. Leave what didn't."* (Reconstruction — establishes agency)
- *"You have seen enough. Or you haven't."* (Investigation — respects player autonomy)
- *"You noticed... You noticed... This is what it means to Witness."* (Revelation — synthesizes)

The Iris never says:
- "Click here"
- "You got it right!"
- "Try again"
- "Your score is..."

⚠️ **Observation:** During Investigation phase, the prompt "Tap objects in the moment to attune" is slightly instructional. This could be softened to: *"Each anomaly holds a perspective. Attune to what draws your attention."* — but this is a minor polish opportunity.

**Verdict:** The Iris guides through observation and reflection, not instruction.

### Does Discovery Feel Meaningful?

**Yes.** The reconstruction phase uses a revolutionary mechanic: spatial fragment placement with *no validation*. The player places what they remember. Some fragments go to correct positions. Some don't. It does not matter.

What matters is the *act of remembering*. As the player places fragments, the desk background gradually regains color and clarity — a physical manifestation of memory reconstruction.

The investigation phase deepens this: each attunement reveals a new layer of truth. The player must *choose* which objects to attune to, and each choice reveals something different. When the discovery threshold is reached (3 attunements), the Iris intervenes — but the player is free to continue exploring.

✅ **Verdict:** Discovery is self-directed, meaningful, and personal.

### Does the Reveal Feel Emotional?

**Yes.** The revelation phase builds the archive entry stepwise:
1. **Carried Fragments** — what the player placed (☑) and didn't place (☐)
2. **Attunements** — what perspectives the player explored
3. **Iris Note** — the emotional truth of the moment
4. **Progress** — Witness Progress and mastery (currently uses prototype terminology)

The Iris Note is the emotional climax:
- WM_001: *"The brush paused not in hesitation, but in reverence for the light across the linen."*
- WM_002: *"Every night, the guard touched the mahogany frame where his grandfather's name was etched."*
- WM_003: *"When the bow settled into the velvet, the final note was already safely across the sea."*

These notes reframe everything the player observed into a single, resonant sentence. The player feels: "I understood something that was hidden in plain sight."

⚠️ **Issue Found:** The progress section still uses "Witness Progress" and "Achievement unlocked" — prototype terminology that breaks the emotional tone. **This must be fixed in Phase 3.**

**Verdict:** The reveal is emotionally powerful, undermined only by residual prototype terminology.

## 4. Issues Found

### Critical (Must Fix)
1. **WitnessRevelationScreen.gd** — `"+%d Witness Progress"` and `"Achievement unlocked: %s"` are player-facing prototype terms. Must become "Insight" and a more poetic acknowledgment.
2. **HomeV2Screen.gd** — `"Level %d"` in achievement pill should be "Rank" for consistency with the Witness framing.
3. **ExperiencesScreen.gd** — `"Choose a Challenge Type"` is legacy terminology. This screen is for Discovery mode (not Story Mode), but still player-facing.

### Moderate (Should Fix)
4. **.tscn fallback textures** — `WitnessObservationScreen.tscn`, `WitnessReconstructionScreen.tscn`, and `WitnessInvestigationScreen.tscn` reference `observation_challenge_01.png` as default texture. While the runtime dynamically overrides with moment-specific backgrounds, the fallback should not be a legacy challenge asset.
5. **Investigation instruction text** — "Tap objects in the moment to attune" is slightly too instructional.

### Minor (Nice to Fix)
6. **Awakening title display** — "THE AWAKENING" appears as conventional floating text. Could be more organically integrated.
7. **Fragment sprites** — Reconstruction palette uses emoji icons rather than production visual assets.
